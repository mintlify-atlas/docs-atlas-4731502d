<Warning>
  On 12 May 2022, Thailand's Wildlife Conservation and Protection Committee voted to designate the Blue Whale as **reserved wildlife** — the highest tier of domestic legal protection. This adds it to CITES Appendix I coverage, making any form of international commercial trade absolutely prohibited.
</Warning>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Introduction">
    The Blue Whale is the **largest animal in the history of life on Earth**. Its body is long and streamlined, with a broad, flat head shaped like the letter U when viewed from above. A single longitudinal ridge runs along the top of the head. The back is blue-gray, the belly slightly paler, and both flanks and back are patterned with lighter blue or gray mottling.

    **Conservation status**
    In May 2022, the Thai Wildlife Conservation and Protection Committee formally elevated the Blue Whale to **reserved wildlife** status, granting it the maximum protection available under Thai law. This decision reflects how rare and vulnerable encounters with this species are in Thai waters.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Threats">
    Although no specific stranding or mortality cases involving Blue Whales have been recorded in Thai waters, the species faces the same suite of threats as other rare marine mammals:

    **Bycatch**
    Accidental entanglement in commercial fishing gear (purse seines, trawls, longlines) in feeding areas is the primary concern.

    **Marine pollution**
    - *Plastic ingestion* — Blue Whales can ingest marine debris along with the vast amounts of water they filter for krill.
    - *Chemical toxins* — Pollutants from wastewater accumulate through the food chain.
    - *Noise pollution* — Activities such as petroleum seismic surveys and shipping sonar interfere with the whale's communication and navigation systems, and are associated with stranding events worldwide.

    **Vessel strikes**
    Collisions with large vessels and propeller injuries are documented causes of Blue Whale mortality globally.

    **Natural factors**
    Extreme weather, illness, and natural aging can also contribute to mortality.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Conservation">
    **Legal protection**
    The Blue Whale's elevation to reserved wildlife status in Thailand in May 2022 is the most significant recent conservation milestone for the species in the region. The designation carries strict criminal penalties for hunting, trading, possessing, or harming the animal.

    **Data collection and monitoring**
    The Department of Marine and Coastal Resources (DMCR) continuously tracks sightings through social media monitoring and citizen networks, building a national database of status, habitat use, and migration routes. This data forms the foundation for future conservation planning.

    **Stranding preparedness**
    Thailand maintains a national stranding response manual covering large cetaceans. In the event a Blue Whale is found injured or stranded:
    1. Assess the situation immediately and mobilize a veterinary team.
    2. Apply first aid to support the animal in the water and prevent water from entering the blowhole.
    3. If survival is uncertain, a full necropsy is performed on deceased animals to determine the cause of death and improve future response protocols.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
